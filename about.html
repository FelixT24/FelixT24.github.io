About Felix

I embarked on my coding journey back in 2016 at the New York Institute of Technology, where I dived into the fascinating world of programming using Java. The following year, I transferred to Nassau Community College, and it was there that I truly honed my skills in the Java language. Seeking further knowledge and growth, I transferred once more in 2020, this time to CUNY York College. At York, my focus shifted more towards computer concepts and functionality, although I did receive a basic introduction to HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Python. However, it was through my own self-teaching efforts that I delved deeper into these languages, expanding my proficiency along the way. In 2022, I proudly graduated from York College with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. Eager to continue my programming education, I enrolled in and successfully completed Harvard's CS50 course, where I gained valuable experience in languages such as C, SQL, and Flash. This course also allowed me to further enhance my knowledge of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Python. My passion for the technical field ignited during my early teenage years when I assembled my very first PC build. In my leisure time, you'll find me immersed in my hobbies, which include building PCs, repairing tablets and phones, and indulging in self-improvement books.
